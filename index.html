Organization

vhHacks

Noticing a lack of secondary school representation at local hackathons, I founded vhHacks, one of Greater Vancouver's only hackathons dedicated to high school students. More than one hundred students will gather and collaborate to build and showcase a project in under 24 hours.

Visit vhHacks

Game

A Lone Oak

A Lone Oak was a survival game I began working on in 2016. It uses random terrain generation to create a randomized side-scrolling level for the player to explore. The player obtains resources from a single oak tree that resides in the centre of the world, and must survive until rescue arrives.

View A Lone Oak

About

Andy Zhao


Hi! I'm inho, a high-school student with a huge passion for computer science, programming, graphics design, and hardware.

I started my interest in computers in the spring of 2013. I attended a local spring break camp and learned the basics of coding logic. From there, I began experimenting on my own. Now, I am fluent in GML, Java, and am fairly proficient in C#. Currently, I'm taking second semester courses at Simon Fraser University, learning about computer science theory, programming practices, and C.

Graphics design is another one of my interests. This passion began in school, where I began designing logos, artwork, and all sorts of different things for school events, clubs, or other activities.

To enrich the hacker community in my city, I'm hosting vhHacks, a hackathon for other high school students like myself. Learn more at vhhacks.ca
