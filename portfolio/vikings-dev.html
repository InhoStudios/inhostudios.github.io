The Design: Vikings Dev



Final Icon and Text


The final Vikings Dev logo is the one shown here: a mahogany vikings head enclosed in curly braces. It's used for our schools coding club, and was also adapted for school hoodies.





First Attempt


This is the original emblem we were going to use for Vikings Dev, formerly called Vikings Code, I guess. The arrow emblem was used to symbolize North, although it wasn't a very obvious connection. I also added a weird shadow underneath.





V-Flip


I added a slight alteration to the logo, flipping the previous arrow into a V, standing for Viking. I also removed the shadow, as it didn't look as good flipped, and added an inner glow.





"Should have put a ring on it"


The V logo seemed a little dull, so I tried to add some rings to one side to add some variation. It added some variety, but still didn't look quite right. At this point, I decided to completely redraw the logo.





Rebirth


I completely redrew the logo, and my friend helped me smooth the edges to my viking drawing. The shape was used in the final, but the circuitboard overlay behind and the font was changed, as it seemed cluttered and not quite as clean as I would have liked.





The Hoodie


The Viking head design was borrowed by our schools yearbook team to use in the international student's hoodie designs. They omitted the curly braces (duh) and essentially traced over our original design. However, they also traced over the stroke, which makes the viking look a little bit pudgy, but otherwise it's a nice hoodie. Extra points for giving me a free hoodie.
